Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers to common questions about OpenSource Compass and open source contributions.

What is OpenSource Compass?

OpenSource Compass is a beginner-friendly platform that helps contributors navigate open source programs, best practices, and real-world contribution workflows.

Do I need prior experience?

No. Many open source contributions involve documentation, design, testing, and beginner-friendly issues. Programming experience helps but is not mandatory.

How do I start contributing?

Start by reading the Guides section, pick a beginner-friendly issue, follow the project’s contribution guidelines, and submit a pull request.

Which programs are beginner-friendly?

Programs like Hacktoberfest, Social Winter of Code, and GirlScript Summer of Code are excellent starting points.

Where can I get help?

Most projects have active communities on GitHub, Discord, or Slack. You can also explore our Guides and Resources pages.

Will my contributions remain after a program ends?

Yes. All merged pull requests remain permanently on GitHub and continue to strengthen your public profile and portfolio.
