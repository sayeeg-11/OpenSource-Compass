Code of Conduct

A Code of Conduct (CoC) is a set of rules outlining the social norms, rules, and responsibilities of an individual project, party, or organization. It is essential for creating a safe and inclusive environment for all contributors.

Key Principles

  • Respect: Treat everyone with respect, regardless of their background or experience level.
  • Inclusivity: Welcome and support people of all backgrounds and identities.
  • Harassment-Free: We pledge to make participation in our project a harassment-free experience for everyone.

How to Follow It

Read the project's CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md file before contributing. If you witness or experience unacceptable behavior, report it according to the enforcement guidelines listed in the CoC.

Open Source Licensing

Licenses define how others can use, modify, distribute, and contribute to the software. Choosing the right license is crucial for open source projects.

Common Licenses

  • MIT License: A permissive license that allows users to do almost anything with the code, as long as they provide attribution.
  • Apache 2.0: Similar to MIT but includes an express grant of patent rights from contributors to users.
  • GNU GPLv3: A copyleft license that requires any changes to the code to be released under the same license.

Always respect the license of the project you are contributing to and ensure your contributions are compatible with it.

Inclusive Communication

Clear and respectful communication is the backbone of open source. Whether you are opening an issue, submitting a pull request, or reviewing code, your words matter.

Best Practices

  • Be Kind: Remember there is a human behind every screen.
  • Be Constructive: Focus on the code, not the person. Provide actionable feedback.
  • Use Inclusive Language: Avoid gendered terms (e.g., use "folks" or "everyone" instead of "guys").
  • Assume Positive Intent: Misunderstandings happen. Clarify before reacting.

Responsible Use

Being a good open source citizen means respecting the work of others and the boundaries of maintainers.

Guidelines

  • Credit and Attribution: Always give credit where it is due. If you use someone else's code, acknowledge it.
  • Maintainer Boundaries: Maintainers are often volunteers. robust response times vary. Be patient and do not demand immediate fixes.
  • Give Back: If you use a project heavily, consider contributing back, whether through code, documentation, or sponsorship.
