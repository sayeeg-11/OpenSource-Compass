GirlScript Summer of Code

Large-scale open-source program for students

GirlScript Summer of Code (GSSOC) is a 3-month long open source program designed to inspire students to explore technology and make their first meaningful open-source contribution. Despite the name, GSSOC is open to all genders and welcomes beginners and experts alike.

Official GSSOC Portal

About the Program

GirlScript Summer of Code is a nationwide summer program that connects students with mentors and projects to help them get started in open source. The program runs for 3 months (typically March - May) and features a vast selection of projects across various technologies, making it perfect for beginners and experienced contributors alike.

Why Choose GSSOC?

Discover what makes GirlScript Summer of Code the perfect starting point for your open source journey

Beginner-Friendly

Perfect for newcomers! GSSOC is designed to help students take their first steps in open source with guidance from experienced mentors and a supportive community.

Vast Project Selection

Choose from hundreds of projects across web development, mobile apps, AI/ML, blockchain, and more. Find the perfect project that matches your interests and skills.

Mentorship Support

Get personalized guidance from experienced mentors throughout the program. Learn best practices, code review, and professional development from industry experts.

Real Contributions

Make meaningful contributions to real-world projects. Build your portfolio, improve your coding skills, and gain experience that employers value.

Gamified Experience

Stay motivated with a real-time leaderboard. Earn points for every successful pull request based on the complexity of the task, fostering a healthy competitive spirit.

Technical Workshops

Access exclusive live sessions and webinars conducted by industry experts. Learn about Git, GitHub, and various modern tech stacks to boost your development journey.

Program Timeline

1

Registration Opens

February - March

Register on the official GSSOC portal. Create your profile, explore available projects, and start connecting with project maintainers. This is your time to research and find the perfect project.

2

Project Selection & Application

March

Browse through hundreds of projects and apply to the ones that interest you. Make initial contributions to show your interest and get selected by project maintainers.

3

Coding Period

March - May

The main coding phase begins! Work on your selected projects, make contributions, and collaborate with mentors. Regular evaluations ensure you're on the right track.

4

Evaluation & Results

May - June

Final evaluations take place based on your contributions throughout the program. Top performers receive certificates, swag, and internship opportunities.
